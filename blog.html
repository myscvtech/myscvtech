How to Prep for a Clean AV Install

Posted May 10, 2025 · By SCV Tech

Great installs start with great prep. We check wall type (stud/drywall/brick), power locations, attic/crawl access, and wire paths. Mark TV height and seating distance beforehand; we’ll bring studs, anchors, and in-wall rated cabling to keep everything concealed and code-safe.

Have your streaming logins ready and we’ll label inputs, name zones, and set up your remotes/phone apps so everything is turnkey when we leave.

Protecting Your Business with Smart Security

Posted April 19, 2025 · By SCV Tech

For storefronts and offices, we recommend IP cameras with NVRs (longer retention, better detail), access control at employee/stock entrances, and motion-activated lighting. Mobile alerts and AI detection reduce false notifications while keeping you in the loop from anywhere.

We can integrate cameras with smart locks and schedules so vendors get access only when they should—and you have a full audit trail.

AV Pre-Wiring vs. Retrofitting: What’s Better?

Posted March 30, 2025 · By SCV Tech

Pre-wiring during construction is fastest and most cost-effective long-term: pull extra Cat6 and speaker lines to common zones and rack locations. Retrofitting still works—expect more time for fishing cables and patch/paint. Either way, we label everything and leave a tidy rack that’s easy to service later.

Rule of thumb: wire for today’s needs + at least one spare per location.

Outdoor AV: Weather-Ready Speakers & TVs

Posted March 5, 2025 · By SCV Tech

Backyard movie nights and poolside playlists need gear that handles heat, cold, and moisture. We use weather-rated speakers/TVs, burial-rated wire, and surge protection. Zone control keeps it loud outside and quiet inside.

Add smart lighting along pathways and seating—it’s safer and it looks awesome.

NVRs work with IP cameras and deliver higher resolutions, smarter analytics, and simpler wiring. DVRs pair with analog cameras and are fine for basic coverage. We’ll size storage for your retention goals.
